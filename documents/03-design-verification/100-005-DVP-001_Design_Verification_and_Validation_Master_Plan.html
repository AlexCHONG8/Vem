2. Purpose

The purpose of this Design Verification and Validation Master Plan (V/VMP) is to document the required tests for a formal product related project. This plan lists which tests shall be conducted by SUMMED for design, process and user V/V, as required by the FDA Quality System Regulation (21 CFR 820) and ISO13485:2016. This V/VMP is designed for the medical device constituent of a combination product.

The Verification and Validation activities are driven from input approved at Design Review:

  • The Design Input Requirement (DIR), document no. SM100-005-DIR-001.
  • The User Requirement Specification (URS), document no. 100-005-URS-001.
  • Product Requirement Specification document no. 100-005-PRS-001.
  • Risk Assessment for Management plan, and FMEA (DFMEA, PFMEA).

5. Tracking And Reporting Systems

If there is a test failure, an investigation shall be conducted and documented as part of the test report. Justification must be provided in the test report if the results are still considered valid. If the results of the investigation lead to a change in design, Document and Change Controls (QP01 and QW-RD-03) shall be followed for documentation and traceability purposes.

6. Definitions

  • Design Validation: Establishing by objective evidence that device specifications conform to user needs and intended use(s). Completed under actual or simulated use conditions.
  • Design Verification: Establishing by objective evidence confirming that the design output meets the design input requirements.
  • Process Validation: Demonstrating that a process when operated within specified limits results in product complying with predetermined requirements.
  • Tool/Equipment Validation: Process of checking that a tool or equipment meets specifications and fulfills its intended purpose.
