1. Purpose

To verify auto-injector performance and packaging integrity after simulated transport testing.

2. Scope

This protocol applies to simulated transport testing for SM100-005 adult model auto-injectors.

3. Test Unit

3.1 Front Component Packaging

  • Carton Dimensions: 592 × 392 × 240 mm
  • Carton Weight: 12 kg
  • Contents: 300 Simject front components

3.2 Rear Component Packaging

  • Carton Dimensions: 592 × 392 × 240 mm
  • Carton Weight: 9 kg
  • Contents: 300 Simject rear components

4. Assurance Level

Based on product value and shipping volume, all tests select Assurance Level II.

5. Distribution Cycle

DC-4 + DC6 principle applied.

6. Test Plan

7. Commissioned Testing

7.1 Simulated transport testing is commissioned by Zhejiang Summed Medtech Co., Ltd. (hereinafter "Summed") to Shanghai Guoyan Medical Device Testing Center Co., Ltd.

7.2 Shanghai Guoyan Medical Device Testing Center Co., Ltd. shall provide following raw data:

  • Vibration test PSD spectrum
  • Temperature and humidity curve recording (1-minute interval)
  • Drop shock waveform (peak acceleration, velocity change)

7.3 After simulated transport testing per Section 8 is completed, test samples shall be retrieved by Summed for subsequent functional testing.

8. Functional Testing

8.1 Test Environment Requirements

Test laboratory temperature and humidity must be controlled within 25 ± 3°C and 55 ± 20% RH range.

8.2 Test Laboratory

Functional testing is performed at Summed laboratory. Use front and rear components retrieved after Section 8 simulated transport testing, assembled with batch XX pre-filled syringe (PFS) into finished auto-injectors for functional testing.

8.5 Auto-Injector Assembly

Use front and rear components retrieved after Section 8 simulated transport testing, assembled with batch XX pre-filled syringe (PFS) according to "000-000-TIS-001 Lisa & Lucy Manual Final Assembly Fixture Test Instruction V1.0" to obtain finished auto-injectors.

8.6 Test Quantity

Functional testing includes 30 auto-injectors. During assembly, use front and rear components retrieved after Section 8 simulated transport testing. Sample 30 front and 30 rear components from respective packaging boxes. Ensure sampling 3 units from each layer tray, sampling from all 10 layer trays, and guarantee random and average sampling throughout the process.

8.7 Pre-Test Conditioning

Auto-injectors assembled per Section 8.5 shall be conditioned for at least 4 hours under standard conditions: temperature 23 ± 5°C and relative humidity 50 ± 25% RH before testing.

8.8 Functional Test Items and Methods

No. Test Item Test Method
1 Dose accuracy 100-005-TIS-023 (V1.0)

3 Cap removal force
4 Injection activation force (pressing needle shield)
5 Injection time for 0.3 mL dose

8 Device status indication
9 Needle shielding
10 Injection start audible prompt
11 Sharps injury protection

9. Acceptance Criteria

No. Item Acceptance Criteria
1 Packaging appearance Visual inspection after Section 8:
1. Visual inspection shows outer carton has no obvious structural damage, such as visible holes or tears. Slight dents or impact marks allowed.
2. Visual inspection shows outer carton labels remain clearly readable, no peeling or blurring.
3. Visual inspection shows inner packaging has no obvious displacement or structural damage when outer carton is opened.
2 Product appearance Visual inspection of contents (front and rear components) after Section 8:
1. Visual inspection shows product has no parts ejected or fallen off, maintaining assembly integrity.
2. Visual inspection shows product has no scratches, cracks, or color inconsistencies.

10. Validation Report

10.1 Report shall contain detailed information of used auto-injectors and PFS, including model, batch number, quantity, etc.

10.2 Report shall specify detailed process and data of simulated transport testing and functional testing.

10.3 Report shall include result determination for simulated transport testing. If testing fails, re-testing shall be performed until passed. Each testing process and result shall be recorded and described in detail in the report.

11. Report Content

11.1 Report Requirements

11.1.1 Test Sample Information

Report shall include PFS model, batch number, quantity, and other relevant information.

11.1.2 Test Data

Report shall include complete functional test data and results.

11.1.3 Test Results and Conclusion

Report shall include test result description and conclusion. Result description shall clearly state whether each test item passed or failed.
