2. Purpose

To verify that the Simject-030 bridge (adult) project products still meet performance requirements after undergoing simulated transport testing.

3. Scope

This protocol applies to the simulated transport testing of the Simject-030 bridge (adult) project.

4. Responsibility

  • Analyst: Responsible for drafting this protocol, completing the tests according to the plan, and issuing the test report.
  • R&D Engineer: Responsible for reviewing this protocol.
  • R&D Lead: Responsible for approving this protocol.

6. Definitions

  • Acceptance Criteria: The acceptable quality level that the transport unit must reach after undergoing the test plan.
  • Assurance Level: Test intensity levels based on the probability of occurrence in a typical distribution cycle. Level I is high intensity with low probability; Level III is low intensity with high probability; Level II is in between.
  • Distribution Cycle: A test plan listed in sequence to simulate hazards expected in a specific path from production to consumption.
  • Transport Unit: The smallest complete unit that will undergo the distribution environment, such as a shipping container and its contents.

8. Simulated Transport Test Procedure

8.1 Preconditioning

Testing is conducted under standard atmospheric conditions (23°C ± 1°C / 50% RH ± 2%). Condition for 72 hours or ensure all packaging system components reach equilibrium.

8.2 Test Unit

  • 8.2.1 Simject front component carton: Dimensions 592*392*240mm; Weight 12kg, containing 300 front components.
  • 8.2.2 Simject rear component carton: Dimensions 592*392*240mm; Weight 9kg, containing 300 rear components.

8.3 Assurance Level

Based on product value and shipping volume, all tests select Assurance Level II.

8.4 Distribution Cycle

DC-4 + DC-6 principle applied.

8.5 Test Plan

Seq. Test Plan Remarks
1 Environmental Preconditioning 23±1℃, 50±2%RH, Duration: 72h
2 Plan A: Manual Handling Initial drop test
3 Plan C: Vehicle Stacking Compression test
Plan D: Stacking Vibration Vibration test
Plan E: Vehicle Vibration Vibration test
4 Plan F: Unconstrained Vibration Repeated shock
5 Plan H: Environmental Hazard Not applicable
6 Plan J: Concentrated Impact /
7 Plan A: Manual Handling Final drop test
8 Plan B: Warehouse Stacking /

8.6 Commissioned Testing

8.6.1 The simulated transport testing is commissioned by Summed to Shanghai Guoyan Medical Device Testing Center Co., Ltd.

8.6.2 The testing center shall provide raw data: vibration PSD spectrum; temp/humidity records (1-min interval); drop shock waveform (peak acceleration, velocity change).

8.6.3 After testing per 8.5, samples are retrieved by Summed for subsequent functional testing.

9. Functional Test

9.1 Test Environment Requirements

Lab temperature 25 ± 3°C and humidity 55 ± 20% RH.

9.2 Test Laboratory

Performed at Summed laboratory. Retrieve front and rear components after Section 8 transport testing, assemble with batch XX PFS into finished auto-injectors.

9.5 Auto-Injector Assembly

Retrieve components after transport testing, assemble with batch XX PFS according to manual assembly instructions to obtain finished pens.

9.6 Test Quantity

Test 30 auto-injectors. Sample 30 front/rear components from respective cartons. Ensure sampling 3 units from each blister tray (10 trays total) randomly.

9.7 Pre-Test Conditioning

Place assembled auto-injectors at 23 ± 5°C and 50 ± 25% RH for at least 4 hours before testing.

11. Report

  • 11.1.1 Report shall contain detailed info of pens and PFS (model, batch, qty).
  • 11.1.2 Report shall note simulated transport process and detailed data.
  • 11.1.3 Report shall include result determination; if failed, re-test until passed. Each process/result recorded.
