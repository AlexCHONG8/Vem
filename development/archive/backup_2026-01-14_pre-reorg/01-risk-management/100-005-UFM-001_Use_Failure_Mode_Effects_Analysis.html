Conclusion and Recommendations

Key Findings

  1. High-risk Failure: Delayed symptom recognition (RPN=80) threatens the 99.999% reliability goal.
  2. EMA Compliance Gap: Training Device development is a mandatory requirement and currently missing.
  3. FDA Timeliness Gap: 0.5s injection requirement requires technical breakthrough.

Priority Actions

  • Immediately launch Training Device development (EMA Mandatory).
  • Implement 99.999% reliability engineering (FDA Core Requirement).
  • Establish Prescriber Checklist system (EMA Doctor Training).
  • Optimize Emergency-Use design (Joint FDA/EMA Requirement).

Through systematic UFMEA optimization, achieving user failure probability ≤ 1 × 10⁻⁵ will support Simject auto-injector in reaching FDA 99.999% reliability goals and EMA Training Device requirements.
