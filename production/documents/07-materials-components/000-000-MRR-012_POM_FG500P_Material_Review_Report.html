Section 4: First Aid Measures

Inhalation: Move to fresh air. If breathing has stopped, give artificial respiration. If breathing is difficult, give oxygen. Consult a physician. For inhalation of overheating or combustion fumes, immediately move to fresh air.
Skin Contact: Unlikely to harm skin from contact, but wash skin after use. For contact with molten material, cool skin immediately with cold water. Do not attempt to peel polymer from skin. Seek medical treatment for burns.
Eye Contact: In case of contact, immediately flush eyes with plenty of water for at least 15 minutes. Consult a physician.
Ingestion: No specific interference indicated. Consult a physician if necessary.
Notes to Physician: Treatment should be directed at the control of symptoms and the clinical condition.

Section 5: Fire-Fighting Measures

Extinguishing Media: Water, foam, dry chemical powder, carbon dioxide (CO₂)
Specific Hazards: Combustible. Burns without flame. Large quantities of molten material can ignite spontaneously in air. Water quenching is effective. Minimize dust generation and accumulation. Temperature control system failures in processing equipment such as extruders may cause explosion hazards. Hazardous combustion products may include: Carbon monoxide, Carbon dioxide
Protective Equipment: Wear self-contained breathing apparatus and full protective clothing
Evacuation: Evacuate personnel and stay upwind from fire

Section 6: Accidental Release Measures

Personal Precautions: Spills create slip hazards
Environmental Precautions: Do not discharge into streams, ponds, lakes, or sewers
Cleanup Methods: For fine material spills, sweep slowly or use vacuum. Do not use cleaning methods that may create combustible dust clouds (e.g., compressed air). Do not allow dust to accumulate on surfaces as it may form explosive mixtures if airborne at sufficient concentrations. Use only non-sparking tools.

Section 7: Handling and Storage

Handling: Open containers only in well-ventilated areas. Wash hands thoroughly after handling. Provide adequate ventilation where dryers, mechanical equipment, and dust or vapors are generated. Prevent dust inhalation. Pneumatic conveying and mechanical handling operations can generate combustible dust. Minimize dust generation and accumulation. Establish routine housekeeping to ensure dust does not accumulate on surfaces.
Storage: Vapors from residual polymer monomers may accumulate in the top of closed containers. Store in cool, dry place. Keep containers tightly closed to prevent moisture absorption and contamination. Store in areas equipped with sprinkler systems. Store in well-ventilated area away from heat and direct sunlight.

Section 10: Stability and Reactivity

Chemical Stability: Stable under normal temperature and pressure conditions
Polymerization: Will not undergo polymerization reaction
Conditions to Avoid: Polymer melting temperature: >230°C. Avoid prolonged exposure to or above recommended processing temperatures
Incompatible Materials: Strong acids and bases (decomposes to form formaldehyde) and strong oxidizing agents. At melting temperatures, acetal resin is incompatible with halogenated polymers such as PVC and PVDC and any elastomers containing halogenated polymers. Even trace amounts as contaminants can cause sudden, spontaneous formaldehyde gas generation. Workplace formaldehyde concentrations may far exceed threshold levels. Can cause unsafe pressurization of equipment such as extruders and molds. Do not contaminate virgin resin or regrind. Do not mix this resin with pigments or additives other than those specified by the company. Do not mix this grade with other acetal grades or other resins without consulting the company. Any of the above may alter thermal stability and lead to decomposition.
Hazardous Decomposition Products: Decomposition depends on duration of exposure to elevated temperatures and pressure. At recommended processing temperatures, significant decomposition should not occur within 30 minutes. Decomposition may be accelerated by contaminants, pigments, and/or other additives. Due to pressurized steam, rapid decomposition can occur during autoclave sterilization - use only minimum time. Allow autoclave to cool completely before opening. Hazardous thermal decomposition products may include: Formaldehyde, Carbon monoxide, Carbon dioxide
