# Failure Mode and Effect Analysis FMEA Instructions

# Requirements:

1. Participants and dates for performed FMEAs must be documented.

2. The customer must be defined: end-user, subsequent operation, the operation itself. As a general rule, in the design FMEA the customer is viewed as the end user, but in the process FMEA the customer is viewed as the next operation in line.

3. The FMEA report shall be signed off by members of staff who were involved in the implementation of the relevant report. More specifically, the author of the FMEA report and the QA project engineer shall imperatively sign off the report; the third signature may be that of a staff from the Automation dept e.g. commissioner or design engineer, or Production dept. The names and titles are not shown on the cover page of the FMEA form as the FMEA team composition depends on the project and process.

# Table content:

Reviewled item: The team shall decide how detailed the analysis will be: less detailed analysis for any system having a mature design, good reliability, maintainability and safety record, more detailed analysis for any newly designed system or system with unknown reliability history. Design level: list item or function of the item being analyzed; Process level: list process or operation being analyzed.

Failure mode: How could this system, design, component, or process fail? For each design/process function, list all failures corresponding to the loss of function such as no dose or no click for auto-injectors.

Cause(s) of failure: How could the failure occur? List as many causes as possible. These causes may be transferred to the downstream FMEA (design to process, process to end-user) as potential failures modes.

Potential effect(s) of failure: What consequence will result from the failure mode when it occurs? Describe the effects of the failure mode on the customer(s). (At a later time, the risk management team shall review the FMEA from a "hazard" perspective)

Current controls: How do we control the design or process to prevent or detect a failure? These controls can be design controls such as design evaluations, mathematical studies, and/or feasibility reviews, and process controls such as fixture error-proofing, tolerance analysis, and/or post-process evaluation.

Rating guidelines: The rating is based on a 1 to 5 scale described in the "FMEA Ratings" working sheet of this document. Take into consideration the current controls when rating the risk level.

RPN: The Risk Priority Number (RPN) is the product of frequency of occurrence (OCC), severity (SEV), and detection (DET) rankings. The threshold of pursuing failures is an RPN equal or greater than 27; however, further risk reducing measures may be taken to improve the process where the RPN is below 27 but where changes could be beneficial to the process.

Recommended action: Describe what you intend to do such as design and/or process revisions, changes to the current control system. After the actions are incorporated in the design or process, the FMEA team should re-evaluate the consequences of occurrence, severity, and detection, and calculate a new RPN. Only a design and/or process revision can bring about a reduction in the severity ranking. If the new RPN equals or exceeds 27, further action will be necessary. If no actions are taken, leave the related columns blank.

Action taken: After the action has been taken, the effective date or completion date with a brief description of the action should be entered. Just because something was recommended, doe done.

Hazard; This column will be used by the risk management (RM) team to identify which failures require to be evaluated in the Risk Management: Risk Estimation & Evaluation Report.
